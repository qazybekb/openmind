# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

OpenMind is meant to be a welcoming project for learners, contributors, and Berkeley students. Please be respectful, constructive, and kind.

Examples of expected behavior:

- Give feedback on code and ideas, not on people
- Assume good intent and ask clarifying questions
- Share context when reporting bugs or proposing changes
- Keep discussions welcoming to newcomers

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Doxxing or sharing private information without permission
- Discriminatory language or exclusionary behavior
- Deliberate disruption of discussions or review threads

## Enforcement

The maintainer may remove comments, close threads, or block contributors who violate this code of conduct.

## Reporting

If someone is making the project feel unsafe or hostile, contact the maintainer privately rather than escalating in public.
